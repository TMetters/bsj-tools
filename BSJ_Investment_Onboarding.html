Why Should You Invest?
Your BSJ salary is a starting point, not a destination. Here's why sitting on cash is the riskiest thing you can do.
The uncomfortable truth: If your money is sitting in a bank account earning 1–2% interest, you are losing purchasing power every year. Indonesian inflation runs at 2–4% annually. UK inflation has averaged 3–4% over the last decade. You are getting poorer in real terms.
💡 The Power of Compounding
Compounding is when your returns generate their own returns. A $10,000 investment at 10% annual return becomes $67,275 in 20 years — without adding another penny. At 15%, it becomes $163,665. Time in the market is your most powerful asset.
📊 BSJ Context — Where You Actually Are
On a BSJ salary of ~$7,000–7,300/month, most teachers have enough to invest meaningfully after living costs. The question isn't whether you can afford to invest — it's whether you can afford not to. Jakarta's low cost of living relative to salary is a genuine advantage most expats underuse.
The goal: Build passive income streams that eventually replace your need to work. Even $500/month in passive income changes your options significantly. $3,000–5,000/month gives you genuine freedom.
⚠️ What Investing Is NOT
Investing is not gambling — though it can become that without discipline. It is not a get-rich-quick scheme. It is not complicated once you understand the basics. And it is not something to defer until you feel "ready." The best time to start was 10 years ago. The second best time is now.

₿ Crypto — The BSJ Teacher Advantage
Crypto is high risk but has produced the highest returns of any asset class over the last decade. Bitcoin specifically has outperformed every other asset over 1, 3, 5, and 10 year periods. The key is position sizing — never put more in crypto than you can afford to lose entirely, but don't put so little that a 10x move doesn't change your life.
📈 Yield-Generating Assets
Some assets pay you while you hold them. MSTY pays monthly distributions. Staking SOL or ETH on Binance generates ~5–8% APY. Government bonds pay fixed interest. These are the building blocks of passive income — the goal is to build a portfolio that pays you monthly without selling anything.
Diversification reality: Owning 5 different cryptocurrencies is NOT diversification — they are all highly correlated. True diversification means holding assets that move independently: stocks, crypto, bonds, and real assets (property, gold) respond differently to economic events.

Your Risk Profile
Before you invest a single dollar, you need to know yourself as an investor. Answer honestly.
🎯 The Three Questions

1. Time horizon: When will you need this money? If the answer is less than 3 years, keep it in cash or low-risk assets. If 5+ years, you can take more risk — time smooths volatility.

2. Income stability: BSJ employment is relatively stable. You have a fixed salary, no dependents on your investment income (yet), and low fixed costs in Jakarta. This means you can tolerate more risk than a freelancer or someone with high fixed expenses.

3. Emotional tolerance: Can you watch your portfolio drop 50% and not panic-sell? Bitcoin dropped 80% in 2018 and 65% in 2022 — people who held are wealthy. People who sold at the bottom locked in permanent losses.

BSJ Teacher Profile (typical): Stable income, low living costs, 10–20 year horizon, moderate-to-high risk tolerance. This profile suits a portfolio weighted toward growth assets (crypto, equities) with some yield generation (MSTY, staking).

Setting Up: Binance
Binance is the world's largest crypto exchange. This is where you'll buy BTC, ETH, SOL and stake your holdings.
Note: Binance is not officially available in Indonesia but is widely used by expats via VPN. Use a reputable VPN (NordVPN, ExpressVPN) and connect to a Singapore or UK server before accessing Binance.
  • 1
    Create your account
    Go to binance.com (via VPN). Click Register. Use your personal email — not your BSJ email. Choose a strong, unique password and store it in a password manager.
  • 2
    Complete KYC verification
    Binance requires identity verification (Know Your Customer). Submit your passport photo and a selfie. This takes 10–30 minutes. Use your British passport for the cleanest verification experience.
  • 3
    Enable 2FA (Google Authenticator)
    Download Google Authenticator on your phone. Go to Security → Two-Factor Authentication → Google Authenticator. Scan the QR code. Never disable this — it's your primary account protection.
  • 4
    Deposit funds
    The easiest method from Indonesia: buy USDT on Indodax with IDR, then transfer USDT to your Binance wallet. Alternatively, use a credit/debit card (higher fees). Avoid bank transfer directly — it's complex from Indonesia.
  • 5
    Make your first purchase
    Go to Trade → Spot. Search BTC/USDT. Start with a small amount ($50–100) to learn the interface. Use a Limit order rather than Market order to control your entry price.
  • 6
    Set up Earn / Staking
    Go to Earn → Simple Earn. You can stake ETH (~3–4% APY) and SOL (~6–8% APY) directly on Binance. This generates passive income on holdings you plan to hold long-term anyway.
Security rules: Never share your seed phrase or private keys with anyone. Binance staff will never ask for your password. Enable withdrawal whitelist — only your known wallet addresses can receive funds. Use the Binance app only on your personal devices.

Setting Up: Indodax
Indodax is Indonesia's largest crypto exchange. It's your bridge between IDR and crypto — essential if you're earning in rupiah.
Why Indodax? Indodax accepts IDR bank transfers directly, which makes it the easiest way to convert your rupiah salary into crypto. You can buy USDT on Indodax and transfer it to Binance for a wider range of assets and lower trading fees.
  • 1
    Create your account
    Go to indodax.com. Register with your email. You will need your Indonesian KTP or passport for verification. The process takes 1–2 business days for approval.
  • 2
    Link your Indonesian bank account
    Go to Profile → Bank Account. Add your BCA, Mandiri, BNI, or other Indonesian bank. This is how you deposit IDR. Transfers are processed within a few hours during business hours.
  • 3
    Deposit IDR and buy USDT
    Transfer IDR from your bank to your Indodax account. Once confirmed, go to Markets → USDT/IDR and buy USDT. This is your stable base currency for moving funds to Binance.
  • 4
    Transfer USDT to Binance
    In Indodax, go to Withdraw → USDT. Enter your Binance USDT deposit address (found in Binance → Wallet → Deposit → USDT → TRC20 network). Always double-check the network — use TRC20 for lowest fees.
  • 5
    Government bonds (optional)
    Indodax also offers Indonesian government bonds (ORI, SBR). These are low-risk IDR-denominated instruments paying 5–7% annually. A useful home for any IDR you want to keep in Indonesia long-term.
Monthly workflow: Salary arrives → transfer portion to Indodax → buy USDT → transfer to Binance → buy your chosen assets. This takes about 15 minutes once set up. Automate what you can.

Your First Investment
Practical guidance on what to buy first, how much, and the habits that separate successful investors from everyone else.
🏁 Start Simple
Your first investment should be Bitcoin. Not because it's the most exciting, but because it's the most understood, the most liquid, and the most proven store of value in crypto. Once you have Bitcoin and understand how it behaves, you can diversify into ETH, SOL, and others.
📅 Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)
DCA means investing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of price. Instead of trying to time the market (which professionals consistently fail to do), you invest $200 every month whether BTC is at $50,000 or $80,000. Over time, this averages your cost basis and removes emotion from the equation. It is the single most effective strategy for most investors.
Suggested starting allocation (moderate risk): 50% BTC, 25% ETH, 15% SOL, 10% cash reserve. As your confidence grows and you understand each asset, adjust. Never invest money you need in the next 12 months.
🔒 The Habits That Matter

1. Never sell in a panic. Every major crypto crash has been followed by a new all-time high. The losers are people who sold.

2. Keep records. Track every purchase date, amount, and price. You'll need this for tax purposes and to understand your actual returns.

3. Don't check prices hourly. Set a weekly review time. Obsessive price-checking leads to poor decisions.

4. Cold storage for significant holdings. Once you hold more than $5,000 in crypto, move the majority to a hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor). Exchange hacks do happen.

Never do this: Don't invest borrowed money. Don't buy obscure altcoins based on Telegram tips. Don't share your account details or seed phrase with anyone. Don't invest your emergency fund.
