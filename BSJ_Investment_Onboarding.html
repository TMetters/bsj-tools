Why You Need to Invest

Keeping money in a savings account feels safe. The numbers say otherwise.

⏱ 8 minutes 📖 Reading + reflection

The invisible tax on your savings

Every year, inflation quietly reduces what your money can buy. In Indonesia, annual inflation has averaged around 3–4% over the past decade. Globally, the US dollar — which many international salaries are benchmarked against — has lost significant purchasing power too.

If your savings sit in a standard Indonesian bank account earning 2–3% interest, and inflation runs at 3–4%, you are effectively losing money in real terms every single year — even while your balance technically grows.

The compounding argument

Compounding is the process of earning returns on your returns. It starts slowly and then accelerates dramatically. The single most important variable is time — which is why starting now matters far more than starting perfectly.

The numbers speak for themselves Someone who invests $500/month at an average 10% annual return, starting at age 30, will have approximately $1.13 million by age 60. Starting at 40 instead yields only $380,000 — less than a third — for the same monthly contribution.

What "financial independence" actually means

Financial independence does not mean being rich. It means your passive income covers your living expenses, so work becomes a choice rather than a necessity. For most BSJ teachers, a realistic passive income target is around $3,000–6,000/month, depending on your lifestyle.

Achieving that through investments is entirely realistic over a 15–20 year horizon if you start now — even on a teacher's salary.

The BSJ advantage Living in Jakarta with a package that includes housing and health benefits means your cost of living is significantly lower than your income. That gap — the difference between what you earn and what you spend — is your most powerful financial tool. It is investable capital.
If your bank account earns 3% interest and inflation runs at 4%, what is happening to your real purchasing power?
Correct. Real return = nominal interest rate minus inflation. At 3% interest and 4% inflation, your money buys 1% less each year. This is why a savings account alone is not a strategy.
⚠ A word of honesty Investing carries risk. Markets go up and markets go down. Nothing in this guide is financial advice — it is education. Before committing significant funds, speak to a licensed financial advisor or, at minimum, do thorough research on each product.

The Asset Landscape

Not all investments are equal. Understanding your options is the first step.

⏱ 10 minutes 📊 Overview + allocation

The main asset classes

An asset class is a category of investment with similar characteristics and behaviour. Every serious investor holds a mix — the exact proportions depend on your age, goals, and risk tolerance.

A starter allocation for a BSJ teacher

There is no universal correct allocation. But for a teacher in their 30s–40s, with a stable income, a long time horizon, and no immediate liquidity needs, the following broad split is a reasonable starting point for discussion with a financial advisor:

Illustrative allocation — not a recommendation 40% Global ETFs (e.g. VT, QQQ via IBKR) · 20% Bitcoin (held on Binance/Indodax) · 20% Income ETFs (e.g. dividend funds) · 10% Cash buffer · 10% Flexible / opportunities

ETFs: the easiest entry point

An ETF (exchange-traded fund) is a basket of stocks or other assets, traded like a single share. Rather than picking individual companies, you buy the whole basket. The most widely recommended for long-term investors are broad global funds.

→
VT — Vanguard Total World Stock ETF
Owns ~9,000 companies across 50+ countries. Maximum diversification in one fund. Extremely low cost (0.07% annual fee).
→
SCHD — Schwab US Dividend Equity ETF
High-quality US dividend payers. Combines income with growth. Popular with financially independent investors.
→
QQQ — Invesco Nasdaq-100 ETF
Top 100 Nasdaq companies — predominantly tech and growth. Higher volatility than VT but stronger historical returns.
What is the key advantage of an ETF over buying individual company shares?
Correct. An ETF gives you exposure to hundreds or thousands of companies in a single purchase. If one company fails, its impact on the whole fund is minimal. This is diversification — and it is the foundation of sensible long-term investing.

The Case for Bitcoin

Why this digital asset deserves a place in a serious investor's portfolio.

⏱ 12 minutes ₿ Deep dive

What Bitcoin actually is

Bitcoin is a decentralised digital currency — a form of money that exists on a global, distributed network with no central authority (no government, no bank) controlling it. It was created in 2009 and has a hard-coded maximum supply of 21 million coins, ever.

That fixed supply is fundamental to its value proposition. Every major fiat currency (USD, IDR, GBP) can be printed by governments in unlimited quantities, which erodes purchasing power over time. Bitcoin cannot be printed.

Think of it as digital gold Gold has been a store of value for millennia because it is scarce, durable, portable, and hard to counterfeit. Bitcoin shares those properties in digital form — with the additional advantages of being verifiably scarce (you can check the code), globally transferable in minutes, and divisible to eight decimal places.

The halving cycle

Approximately every four years, the rate at which new Bitcoin is created (the "mining reward") is cut in half. This event is called the halving. Each halving reduces the new supply entering the market. Historically, halvings have preceded significant price appreciation as demand holds steady or grows while supply contracts.

2012
1st halving — $12 → $1,200
2016
2nd halving — $650 → $20,000
2020
3rd halving — $8,500 → $69,000
2024
4th halving — cycle ongoing
⚠ Past performance is not a guarantee The halving cycles above illustrate historical patterns. Bitcoin has also had drawdowns of 70–85% from peak to trough. Only invest what you can afford to hold through a prolonged downturn.

Institutional adoption

A significant development in 2024 was the approval of spot Bitcoin ETFs in the United States, allowing pension funds, insurance companies, and retail brokers to buy Bitcoin directly through regulated products. This opened the asset to trillions of dollars in institutional capital.

Companies such as MicroStrategy hold Bitcoin as a primary treasury reserve. Countries including El Salvador have adopted it as legal tender. The institutional legitimisation of Bitcoin over the past three years has fundamentally changed the risk profile of the asset.

How much to allocate

Most financial commentators who accept Bitcoin as a legitimate asset class suggest an allocation of 1–10% of a portfolio, depending on risk tolerance. A common starting point for a cautious investor is 2–5%.

The logic: even if Bitcoin went to zero (which remains a possibility, though declining in probability), a 5% allocation would reduce your total portfolio by 5% — a recoverable loss. If Bitcoin performs even moderately well, that 5% can meaningfully improve overall returns.

Why does Bitcoin's fixed supply of 21 million coins matter to its value?
Correct. Scarcity is the foundation of Bitcoin's value proposition. If demand grows over time and supply is fixed, basic economics suggests price rises. This is the opposite of fiat currencies, where supply can be expanded by governments at will.

Setting Up Your Accounts

Step-by-step guides for the three platforms most relevant to BSJ teachers in Indonesia.

⏱ 15 minutes 🔧 Practical setup

You will need accounts on at least two platforms: one Indonesian exchange (for easy IDR deposits) and one global platform (for broader asset access). IBKR is optional but excellent for ETFs.

1. Indodax
2. Binance
3. IBKR
Start here — easiest for Indonesians Indodax is Indonesia's largest regulated crypto exchange. IDR deposits via bank transfer, KTP verification, Indonesian-language support. Ideal first step.
1
Create your account
Go to indodax.com or download the Indodax app (iOS/Android). Click "Daftar" and register with your email address and a strong password.
💡 Use a unique password you do not use anywhere else. Enable Google Authenticator immediately after registering.
2
Complete KYC verification
Go to Settings → Verifikasi. Upload a photo of your KTP (Indonesian national ID) and a clear selfie. Verification typically takes 1–3 business days.
3
Link your bank account
Go to Keuangan → Rekening Bank. Add your Indonesian bank account (BCA, Mandiri, BNI, and most others are supported). This is used for IDR deposits and withdrawals.
4
Make your first deposit
Go to Keuangan → Deposit IDR. Transfer any amount from your bank (minimum usually IDR 10,000). Funds appear within minutes for instant transfer banks.
5
Make your first purchase
Start with BTC (Bitcoin) or USDT (a USD-pegged stablecoin). Go to the market, enter your IDR amount, and confirm the trade. Consider DCA — buying a fixed amount weekly rather than all at once.
💡 DCA (Dollar-Cost Averaging) means buying the same amount regardless of price. It removes the pressure of "timing the market" and is widely recommended for beginners.
The global exchange — more assets, more features Binance is the world's largest crypto exchange by volume. Wider selection of assets, staking features, and lower fees than Indodax. Slightly more complex to set up.
1
Register at binance.com
Go to binance.com and click Register. Use your email or phone number. Note: use the international version, not binance.us (which is US-only).
2
Enable 2FA immediately
Before doing anything else: go to Security → 2-Factor Authentication. Enable Google Authenticator. Write down your backup seed phrase on paper and store it safely. This protects your account from unauthorised access.
⚠ If you lose access to your 2FA and seed phrase, Binance cannot recover your account. The paper backup is essential.
3
Complete KYC
Go to Profile → Identification. Upload your passport (recommended over KTP for international accounts). Verification takes 1–24 hours.
4
Fund your account
Easiest method: buy USDT on Indodax first, then withdraw USDT from Indodax to your Binance deposit address. Use the TRC20 (Tron) network — fastest and cheapest for USDT transfers.
💡 Always double-check the receiving address before sending. Crypto transfers cannot be reversed.
5
Explore staking and Earn
Once funded, look at Binance Earn → Simple Earn. You can earn interest on USDT (typically 3–8% APR) while you decide where to invest. SOL staking via "SOL Staking" gives you native staking rewards.
For stocks and ETFs — requires patience to set up Interactive Brokers (IBKR) is one of the world's best platforms for buying global ETFs at very low cost. Note: IBKR is technically restricted in Indonesia by OJK (the financial regulator). Many expats access it via a VPN — do your own research on the implications.
1
Apply at interactivebrokers.com
Go to interactivebrokers.com → Open Account. Choose "Individual Account". The application takes about 20–30 minutes. You will need your passport, proof of address (utility bill or bank statement), and employment details.
2
Wait for approval
Approval typically takes 1–2 weeks. IBKR may request additional documentation. This is normal — they are a regulated broker operating globally.
3
Fund with a USD wire transfer
Once approved, fund your account via international wire transfer from your USD account (or convert IDR at your bank first). Minimum deposit is $0, but practical minimum to make fees worthwhile is around $500.
4
Buy your first ETF
Search for VT (Vanguard Total World ETF) in the IBKR Trader Workstation or mobile app. Place a market or limit order. Commission is typically $0–1 per trade. This is world-class infrastructure at minimal cost.
💡 Start with VT or SCHD. Both are beginner-friendly, extremely well-diversified, and have very low annual fees.
